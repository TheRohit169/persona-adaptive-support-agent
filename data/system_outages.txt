SYSTEM OUTAGES - INCIDENT RESPONSE AND CUSTOMER COMMUNICATION
==============================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
This document describes how YourSaaS Product handles system outages, communicates with affected customers, and what customers can expect during and after incidents.

STATUS PAGE
-----------
Real-time system status: https://status.yoursaasproduct.com
Subscribe to updates: https://status.yoursaasproduct.com/subscribe

Status levels displayed on the status page:
  - Operational: All systems functioning normally
  - Degraded Performance: Partial slowdown affecting a subset of users
  - Partial Outage: Core features unavailable for some users or regions
  - Major Outage: Critical systems unavailable for all or most users
  - Under Maintenance: Planned maintenance window in progress

INCIDENT SEVERITY LEVELS
-------------------------

P0 - Critical:
  Definition: Complete service unavailability or data integrity risk
  Response time: 15 minutes (detection to public acknowledgment)
  Resolution target: 2 hours
  Communication: Every 30 minutes via status page + email + in-app banner

P1 - High:
  Definition: Core feature unavailable for >20% of users
  Response time: 30 minutes
  Resolution target: 4 hours
  Communication: Every 60 minutes via status page + email

P2 - Medium:
  Definition: Feature degradation, minor errors, or isolated impact
  Response time: 2 hours
  Resolution target: 24 hours
  Communication: Status page update only

P3 - Low:
  Definition: Cosmetic issues or edge case bugs with no functional impact
  Response time: 24 hours
  Resolution target: Next release sprint
  Communication: Release notes

PLANNED MAINTENANCE WINDOWS
----------------------------
Scheduled maintenance occurs on:
  - Tuesdays, 02:00 – 04:00 UTC (infrastructure updates)
  - Last Sunday of each month, 00:00 – 06:00 UTC (major version deployments)

Advance notice:
  - 72-hour notice for maintenance affecting core features
  - 7-day notice for maintenance exceeding 2 hours

WHAT TO DO DURING AN OUTAGE
------------------------------

For Users:
  1. Check https://status.yoursaasproduct.com first
  2. Subscribe to incident updates for real-time notifications
  3. Avoid repeated login attempts (they won't resolve platform-side issues)
  4. Save any unsaved work locally if the application is partially responsive
  5. Wait for the all-clear update before retrying critical operations

For API Integrations:
  1. Implement circuit breaker logic to stop sending requests during outages
  2. Queue non-critical operations locally and replay after restoration
  3. Monitor the /v1/health endpoint for automated detection:
     GET https://api.yoursaasproduct.com/v1/health
     Response: {"status": "healthy|degraded|down", "timestamp": "..."}
  4. Set appropriate timeout values (recommended: 30 seconds connect, 60 seconds read)

POST-INCIDENT REPORTS
----------------------
After P0 and P1 incidents, a Post-Incident Report (PIR) is published within 72 hours at:
https://status.yoursaasproduct.com/incidents

PIRs include:
  - Incident timeline
  - Root cause analysis
  - Impact summary (users affected, duration)
  - Remediation steps taken
  - Preventive measures to avoid recurrence

SLA CREDITS FOR OUTAGES
-----------------------
Customers on Professional and Enterprise plans are eligible for SLA credits:
  - Downtime of 0.1% – 1%: 10% credit on monthly invoice
  - Downtime of 1% – 5%: 25% credit on monthly invoice
  - Downtime >5%: 50% credit on monthly invoice

Calculating downtime:
  Monthly SLA target: 99.9% uptime = max 43.8 minutes downtime per month
  Credits are applied automatically to the next billing cycle
  Submit a credit request if not automatically applied: billing@yoursaasproduct.com

DISASTER RECOVERY
-----------------
RTO (Recovery Time Objective): 2 hours for P0 incidents
RPO (Recovery Point Objective): Maximum 15 minutes of data loss in catastrophic scenarios
Backup frequency: Continuous for transactions; hourly for application state
Geo-redundancy: Active-active across US-East, US-West, EU-West, and AP-Southeast regions
